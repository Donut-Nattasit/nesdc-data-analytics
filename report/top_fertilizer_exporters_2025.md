# Global Fertilizer Exporters Ranking 2025

This brief presents the top 10 exporters of fertilizers (HS Chapter 31: Fertilizers) in the year 2025, using trade records from the Global Trade Atlas (GTA) database. 

Due to differences in reporting practices—namely, that major fertilizer exporters such as the Russian Federation (`RU`) and Belarus (`BY`) do not report direct export transactions to the database—ranking exporters solely on direct reported exports leaves significant gaps in the global picture. To address this, we present two distinct methodologies:

1. **Direct Reported Exports**: Aggregated strictly from transactions where the exporting country is the reporting entity (`Trade_Direction = 'Export'`).
2. **Mirror Import Statistics**: Aggregated from import transactions reported by partner countries (`Trade_Direction = 'Import'` where the exporter is the `Partner_ISO`), which captures indirect trade flows and reveals trade activities for non-reporting nations.

## Key Takeaways

- **Russia's Market Dominance**: When using mirror statistics, **Russia** is the world's largest fertilizer exporter in 2025, with an estimated export value of **$18.60 billion USD**. This is significantly ahead of **China**, which ranks second at **$14.42 billion USD**.
- **China and Canada as Leading Reporters**: Under direct reporting, **China** ranks first ($13.49 billion USD) followed by **Canada** ($7.34 billion USD) and **Morocco** ($7.14 billion USD).
- **Statistical Discrepancies**: Discrepancies between direct and mirror values for reporting countries (e.g., China's direct export of $13.49B vs. mirror export of $14.42B) arise due to CIF (Cost, Insurance, and Freight) vs. FOB (Free on Board) valuation differences, shipping lags, and varying reporting coverage among partner countries.
