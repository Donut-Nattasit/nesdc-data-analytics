# Thailand Price Scenarios Summary (2024-2027)

## Executive Summary
This report summarizes the projected impact of varying oil price scenarios on Thailand's price stability, covering the Consumer Price Index (CPI), Export Price Index (ExPI), and Import Price Index (ImPI). The analysis highlights a significant divergence in inflation outcomes starting in 2026, contingent on the trajectory of Dubai Crude prices.

## Analytical Commentary

### 1. Short-term Price Stability (2024-2025)
The Thai economy maintained a low-inflation environment during 2024 and 2025. Headline inflation averaged 0.40% in 2024, followed by a slight technical deflation of -0.13% in 2025. This period was characterized by stable domestic demand and the dissipation of previous supply-side shocks.

### 2. Oil Price Impact on Inflation (2026-2027)
The sensitivity of the Thai price system to energy costs becomes apparent in the 2026-2027 forecast period:
- **Base Case ($110/bbl)**: Inflation is expected to trend upwards, reaching 3.95% by 2027. This reflects a moderate pass-through of energy costs to the broader consumer basket.
- **High-Risk (Bull) Scenario ($115/bbl)**: If Dubai Crude averages $115/bbl, Thailand could face severe inflationary pressure, with CPI growth accelerating to 8.22% in 2027. This surge is primarily driven by the "fuel-to-food" pass-through and transportation costs.
- **Benign (Bear) Scenario ($90/bbl)**: Lower energy costs would successfully anchor inflation expectations, keeping the annual average at a manageable 1.39% in 2027, well within the central bank's target range.

### 3. Trade Price Dynamics and Terms of Trade
The forecast indicates that the **Import Price Index (ImPI)** is significantly more responsive to oil price hikes than the **Export Price Index (ExPI)**. 
- In the Bull scenario, ImPI rises to 142.76 by 2027, while ExPI remains relatively stagnant or slightly declines due to potential global demand softening. 
- This divergence suggests a risk of **Terms of Trade (ToT) deterioration**, where the cost of imports rises faster than the value of exports, potentially weighing on the current account balance and domestic purchasing power.

## Conclusion
The outlook for Thailand's price stability is heavily contingent on global energy market developments. While the current environment remains stable, a sustained rise in oil prices beyond $110/bbl poses a substantial risk of inflationary overshoot and economic pressure through 2027.
