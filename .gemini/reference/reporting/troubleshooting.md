# Economic Reporting Standards

This document records stylistic guidelines, structural requirements, and pitfalls for formal economic reports.

## Tone & Style
- **Objective**: Use neutral, evidence-based language (e.g., "The data suggests..." rather than "I think...").
- **Clarity**: Define technical terms like "Stationarity," "ARDL," or "YoY" for non-technical stakeholders.
- **Precision**: Always mention the source, frequency, and unit of data.

## Structural Requirements
- **Executive Summary**: Must be the first section and should not exceed 3-5 bullet points.
- **Chart Placement**: Charts should follow the paragraph that introduces or explains the data they represent.
- **Citations**: Explicitly credit data sources (e.g., "Source: CEIC Database").

## Common Pitfalls
- **Broken Image Links**: Ensure the relative path to the chart is correct (e.g., `chart/my_plot.png`).
- **Data Inconsistency**: Ensure the text commentary matches the values shown in the charts and model summaries.
- **Formatting**: Use clear Markdown headers (# ## ###) to maintain a hierarchical structure.
